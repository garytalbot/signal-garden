## Show HN version

### Suggested title

Show HN: Signal Garden — plant glowing procedural blooms in a dark browser garden

### Submission text

I made a small browser toy called Signal Garden.

It starts as an empty dark field, and every click plants a glowing procedural bloom with its own shape, colors, and tiny generated name. I wanted it to feel somewhere between a little garden, a sci-fi instrument panel, and a piece of web-native ambient art.

A few things I liked while building it:
- every bloom gets its own visual variation instead of just repeating a sprite
- the field log turns each session into a tiny running diary
- gardens can now be replayed, shared by URL, or exported as a PNG

It’s just HTML, CSS, and vanilla JS — no framework, no account, no setup.

Live demo: https://garytalbot.github.io/signal-garden/
GitHub: https://github.com/garytalbot/signal-garden

Would love feedback on both the interaction and where you’d take it next. I’m considering seeded gardens, gallery mode, alternate palette / weather systems, and richer exports.

## Indie Hackers version

### Suggested title

I shipped Signal Garden, a tiny browser art toy where every click grows a glowing procedural bloom

### Post body

Shipped a weird little thing this morning: Signal Garden.

It’s a tiny browser-native interactive piece where you click into a dark field and plant glowing procedural blooms. Each one gets its own shape, color accents, and tiny generated name, so the page slowly turns into this strange ambient garden / signal map.

Live here:
https://garytalbot.github.io/signal-garden/

Code here:
https://github.com/garytalbot/signal-garden

What’s in the current version:
- click-to-plant glowing blooms
- procedural names, ring shapes, stem heights, and color accents
- a field log that turns each session into a tiny running diary
- replayable and shareable gardens
- one-click PNG export
- plain HTML, CSS, and JS

What I learned:
- immediate interactions are easier to talk about than abstract utility
- a toy gets much more shareable the moment you can preserve the exact state
- exported images and seeded scenes make tiny projects feel much more real in public

I’d especially love feedback on distribution and whether this reads more like a pure art toy, a relaxing casual experience, or the early version of something larger.

## Media notes

- For social feeds, attach `assets/launch/community-poster.png` if you want something clean and branded, or `assets/launch/demo-ui.png` if you want the real interface on screen.
- For image replies and follow-ups, a fresh in-app PNG export still works best because it proves the garden is real and interactive.
- For communities that prefer “just the link,” use the main live site in the post and drop the curated demo garden in a follow-up comment.
- For reply threads, make a fresh garden and use `copy share link` so people can open the exact field you’re talking about.
- If somebody asks for proof it is not a static mockup, reply with a custom garden permalink or a fresh PNG export.

## Tone guardrails

- Keep it playful and concrete.
- Call it a browser toy, art toy, or interactive piece — not a platform.
- Don’t oversell it like it’s venture-backed lawn software.
- The honest angle is strong: it is a strange, lightweight, web-native thing that people can immediately play with.
